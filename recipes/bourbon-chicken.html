Bourbon Chicken

Description

Bourbon chicken is a dish with New Orleans roots that's easy to make with chicken thigh pieces cooked in a sweet brown sugar, bourbon, ginger, garlic, and soy sauce glaze. Serve with rice for a restaurant-worthy meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds skinless boneless chicken thighs, cut in 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch, divided
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • ¼ cup minced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1/3 cup reduced-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup reduced sodium chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup bourbon (or apple juice)
  • 3 tablespoons packed brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon cider vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon crushed red pepper
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Steps

  1. Step 1: Gather Ingredients.
  2. Step 2: Toss chicken with 1 tablespoon cornstarch in a medium bowl
  3. Step 3: Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high heat; add chicken. Cook and stir until browned; transfer to a plate using a slotted spoon.
  4. Step 4: Heat remaining 1 tablespoon oil in same skillet. Add onion, garlic, and ginger; cook and stir until softened and fragrant, 1 to 3 minutes.
  5. Step 5: Stir remaining 1 tablespoon cornstarch, soy sauce, broth, bourbon, brown sugar, vinegar, and crushed red pepper together in a small bowl.
  6. Step 6: Add sauce mixture to skillet; cook and stir until thickened and bubbly.
  7. Step 7: Return chicken to skillet. Cook and stir until coated in sauce and heated through, about 2 minutes.
  8. Step 8: Serve over rice. Top with green onions.
